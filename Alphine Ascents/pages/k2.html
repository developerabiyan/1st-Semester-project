K2 MOUNTAIN

K2 (also known as Chhogori/Qogir, Ketu/Kechu, and Mount Godwin-Austen) is the second-highest mountain on Earth, after Mount Everest. It is located on Pakistan administered. K2 is the highest point of the Karakoram Range and the highest point in Pakistan.

History :

K2 was finally successfully summited on July 31, 1954, by an Italian team led by Ardito Desio. Team members Lino Lacedelli and Achille Compagnoni reached the summit via the Abruzzi Spur, and the expedition also included Pakistani colonel Muhammad Ata-Ullah, Hunza porter Amir Mehdi, and prominent climber Walter Bonatti.

Techniques :

Climbing K2, the second highest mountain in the world, is a challenging and dangerous endeavor that requires extensive mountaineering experience, physical fitness, and proper equipment. It's important to first gain experience by climbing other high-altitude mountains and to be familiar with technical climbing skills, glacier travel, and avalanche safety. Additionally, it's essential to join an experienced and reputable guiding company, as well as to obtain all necessary permits and permissions. Proper training, acclimatization, and a thorough understanding of the risks involved are crucial for a safe and successful ascent of K2./p>

Hazards :

Mountains have always been a dangerous place for people, and the cold weather alone is enough to kill even the most prepared climbers. K2, however, takes the dangers to a new level. Unlike other mountains, the K2 has very steep stretches that do not have any flat breaks where climbers can rest. The mountain is also prone to avalanches due to the massive snowing that gets worse during winter, the avalanches have been responsible for most of the accidents recorded in the area. Rock falls are also a common occurrence that presents a high risk to the climbers who are always at the mercy of nature. The weather up the mountain is extremely brutal with severe storms and strong winds that can reach speeds of up to 100 miles per hour
